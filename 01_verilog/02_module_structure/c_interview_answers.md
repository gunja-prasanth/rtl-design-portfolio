# Interview Answers

## Q1. What is a module in Verilog?

A module is the fundamental building block of a Verilog design used to describe hardware functionality.

---

## Q2. Why are modules used?

Modules improve readability, reusability, and maintainability of designs.

---

## Q3. Which keywords define a module?

The keywords are:

```verilog
module
endmodule
```

---

## Q4. Can a Verilog design exist without a module?

No. Every Verilog design must be enclosed within a module.

---

## Q5. What are ports in a module?

Ports are communication points through which signals enter or leave a module.

---

## Q6. What are the different types of ports?

* input
* output
* inout

---

## Q7. What is module instantiation?

Module instantiation is the process of using one module inside another module.

---

## Q8. Why is modular design important?

Modular design improves scalability, debugging, code reuse, and maintenance.

---

## Q9. Can one module instantiate another module?

Yes. Complex digital systems are commonly built by instantiating smaller modules inside larger modules.
